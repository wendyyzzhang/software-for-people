Blue Gold

   

Isatis tinctoria

The « Blue Gold» Woad or glastum (Isatis tinctoria), also called " pastel des teinturiers» in the South West of France, is a flowering plant in the Brassicacee family (the mustard family) with a documented history of use as a blue dye.
   It grows in the wild in
  • Southern Europe
  • Central
  • South West Asia
And it was mostly cultivated in the South of France between the 14th and 17th centuries.              At that time, the blue colour was sought-after by royalty and nobility (clothes, symbols, paintings).

Woad

was also cultivated in Italy, Germany, England and Northern France (Picardy). The plant was worth millions and created the wealth of the triangle formed by Toulouse, Albi and Carcassonne. Thanks to the clay-limestone soils and to the southern climate, this area produced the most beautiful shade of blue.
